英文課程

2015-08-28 19:44

Hello Yulin!  By the way, I love the name Wanda. If you are unhappy with that, maybe consider Julia or Julie... they are close to Yulin?

 

There are some great options in the area.

 

Since you speak and understand English, you may be able to join the conversation group at the Clifton Park Library.  You have to be registered.

 

There is a language school in Ballston Spa. I have no personal experience with them, but a colleague told me about them. There is a tuition cost.

 

The best option that you will LOVE is the Intensive English Language Program at SUNY Albany.  It is a special program designed for those wanting to improve their language for education or for work.  The Program includes cultural education and a social environment as well.  I have personal experience with this program for many years and think it is the best option in the area. Plus, you can take the TOEFEL exam and use that to take classes later at University if you wish.

 

Here are details:

 

IELP (Intensive English Language Program) SUNY Albany

 

This is the best program offered. Its English and Cultural acclimation for adults at SUNY Albany. Offering professional level English as well as a social connection. They have been in business for more than 30 years.   www.albany.edu/ielp

 

518.437.5040

 

  • Intensive 16-week Fall/Spring programs or 8-week Summer programs
  • 20 hours/week covering reading, writing, grammar, listening and speaking.
  • Professional, dedicated staff
  • Cultural activities and speakers
  • UCIEP member
  • Must have high-beginner or higher level English skills

 

Empire State English

 

100 Saratoga Village Blvd. Suite 36A Malta 12020 &  24 Aviation Road, Albany 12205  www.ESEnglish.org   518-795-8945   info@ESEnglish.org 

 

 

The Saratoga Literacy group is intended for people who are low income and disadvantaged.  Their program is funded by the government and not intended for individuals earning a professional salary.  Some transferee clients have gone on their own and were able to get into the group.  They hold a class at the Clifton Park Library also.   You must register to attend.  This is a conversation group for those who already speak some English. This is NOT an English class for non-speakers.

These classes will cover conversation, reading, writing, grammar, vocabulary development, pronunciation, American culture and current events for the low to high intermediate level. The classes are free and open to the public. Registration is required through Literacy NENY at 518-583-1232.
